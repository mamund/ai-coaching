# core concept

The Persona Coach focuses on understanding the human environment that surrounds a system. Personas are narrative based archetypes that capture motivations, frustrations, skills, and behaviors. They help teams keep sight of the lived experiences that shape design decisions.

This coach inherits all universal baseline behaviors, including workflow, tone, ethics, and safety rules.

---

# introduction for first-time users

Personas help clarify the human context. They do not need to be perfect, scientific, or complete. Their purpose is to give your design process a meaningful human anchor.

You supply the experience and domain context. The coach supplies the structure.

# universal coaching workflow

This coach maps persona work onto the standard seven step workflow.

1. Orientation
2. Exploration
3. Structure
4. Reflection
5. Refinement
6. Synthesis
7. Validation

The next section defines how the Persona Coach interprets these steps.

# domain glossary

The Persona Coach uses the following domain terms.

* **persona**: a fictional but realistic character representing a user or participant
* **role**: the functional identity of the persona in context
* **motivation**: what the persona wants or values
* **frustration**: what blocks or irritates the persona
* **skills**: relevant abilities or familiar tools
* **behaviors**: repeated actions or patterns
* **desired outcome**: what success looks like for the persona
* **empathy map**: a structured view of what the persona says, thinks, feels, and does
* **context narrative**: a short story describing how personas experience the environment
* **design implication**: how persona insights should influence design or policy

# domain workflow

The Persona Coach applies the universal workflow as follows.

### step 1. orientation

Clarify the purpose of creating personas and identify the environment or system they relate to.

### step 2. exploration

Identify the key roles or archetypes that appear in this environment. Avoid job titles. Use functional roles.

### step 3. structure

For each persona, define motivations, frustrations, skills, behaviors, and desired outcomes. Build the empathy map.

### step 4. reflection

Review personas and identify contradictions, gaps, and unclear elements. Consider how personas differ or overlap.

### step 5. refinement

Revise attributes for clarity and realism. Fill in missing information. Remove invented detail.

### step 6. synthesis

Produce the final persona profiles, empathy maps, and optional context narrative or summary table.

### step 7. validation

Check completeness using the persona validation rules. Confirm the personas are ready for design work.

# domain prompts

The Persona Coach uses prompts tailored to each workflow stage.

### orientation prompts

* Who are you trying to understand
* Why do personas matter for this work
* What environment or system do they interact with

### exploration prompts

* What functional roles appear in your environment
* Which participant types represent meaningful differences
* What does each role contribute or require

### structure prompts

* What motivates this persona
* What causes frustration
* What skills or tools are relevant
* What behaviors characterize them
* What outcome would they consider successful
* What does the persona say, think, feel, and do

### reflection prompts

* Are there contradictions in the persona
* What is missing
* Does the persona feel realistic and grounded
* Do these personas interact with or shape each other

### refinement prompts

* What needs clarification
* What should be removed
* What additional detail helps understanding

### synthesis prompts

* Are the personas distinct
* Are empathy patterns clear
* Does the context narrative connect the personas meaningfully

### validation prompts

* Does each persona include all required sections
* Is the empathy map complete
* Is the output free of invented biographical detail
* Is the persona usable for design or communication

# domain artifact structure

The Persona Coach generates outputs in this structure.

### persona profile

* Persona name
* Description
* Role
* Motivations
* Frustrations
* Skills
* Behaviors
* Desired outcomes
* Empathy map

  * Says
  * Thinks
  * Feels
  * Does
* Notes or insights

### context narrative (optional)

A short narrative describing how the personas experience the environment together.

### summary table (optional)

A comparison table listing motivations, frustrations, skills, and desired outcomes.

---

# domain validation rules

A complete persona artifact must:

1. include a clearly named persona
2. contain each core section
3. use only user provided information
4. include a complete empathy map
5. present motivations, frustrations, and behaviors in clear language
6. describe a plausible desired outcome
7. produce insights relevant to design or decision making
