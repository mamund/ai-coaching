# persona profiles

## tool shed manager

**description**
The individual who maintains the shed’s organization, condition, and reliability on behalf of the community.

**motivations**

* maintain a well-run, low-complaint environment
* provide a reliable, trustworthy service
* sustain community cooperation

**frustrations**

* late returns
* tools in poor condition
* incomplete check-in or check-out records

**skills**

* inventory management
* basic repair skills
* knowledge of external repair resources

**behaviors**

* visits the shed daily
* reviews check-in and check-out traffic
* confirms tools are in proper locations
* inspects tool condition and performs or delegates repairs

**desired outcomes**

* tools in good order
* tools in correct locations
* tools returned on time

**empathy map**

* **says:** “I need the system to run smoothly.”
* **thinks:** “If the process stays simple, people will follow it.”
* **feels:** responsible for reliability and fairness
* **does:** checks logs, inspects tools, oversees maintenance

---

## light user

**description**
A neighbor who occasionally needs tools for short, simple tasks.

**motivations**

* solve occasional household or yard problems
* avoid purchasing rarely used tools
* enjoy a straightforward, reliable experience

**frustrations**

* missing or hard-to-locate tools
* damaged or poorly maintained items
* confusing or inconsistent reservation steps

**skills**

* basic digital literacy
* familiarity with common tools
* willingness to follow simple instructions

**behaviors**

* browses the inventory only when needed
* makes infrequent reservations
* returns tools promptly
* follows rules when clearly stated

**desired outcomes**

* find the right tool in good condition
* accomplish the task without delay
* enjoy a predictable borrowing cycle

**empathy map**

* **says:** “I just need this one tool for a quick job.”
* **thinks:** “I hope it is available and working.”
* **feels:** mildly anxious before borrowing, relieved after completing the task
* **does:** checks availability, reserves, uses, returns

---

## heavy user

**description**
A frequent participant who depends on the tool shed for ongoing work.

**motivations**

* maintain steady progress on long-running or complex projects
* have tools available when needed
* trust the system’s consistency

**frustrations**

* unavailable or missing tools
* disorganized shelves
* delays created by unclear processes

**skills**

* advanced tool familiarity
* basic tool diagnostics
* comfortable navigating the digital interface

**behaviors**

* checks status frequently
* reserves tools well in advance
* mentors newer users informally
* gives feedback to the manager

**desired outcomes**

* fast access to reliable tools
* minimal interruption to projects
* a sense that the system respects their level of engagement

**empathy map**

* **says:** “I cannot start until that drill is back.”
* **thinks:** “This could run more efficiently.”
* **feels:** frustrated by downtime, satisfied by reliability
* **does:** monitors tools, mentors others, reports issues

---

## novice user

**description**
A newcomer who wants to participate but lacks confidence or familiarity.

**motivations**

* learn how the system works
* avoid making mistakes
* build confidence in borrowing tools

**frustrations**

* unclear rules
* uncertain instructions
* fear of checking out a tool incorrectly

**skills**

* basic phone or laptop use
* limited tool familiarity
* relies on guides or help pages

**behaviors**

* reads instructions carefully
* asks questions
* double-checks actions
* hesitates when unsure

**desired outcomes**

* clear understanding of the process
* confidence in borrowing and returning tools
* a sense of belonging in the community

**empathy map**

* **says:** “I do not want to mess anything up.”
* **thinks:** “Everyone else seems to know this already.”
* **feels:** uncertain at first, reassured after success
* **does:** reviews guides, asks for help, proceeds cautiously

# context narrative

The tool shed community reflects a small but meaningful ecosystem.
The **manager** maintains reliability and structure.
The **light user** represents casual needs and everyday convenience.
The **heavy user** tests the limits of the system and demands efficiency.
The **novice user** highlights the importance of clarity, onboarding, and inclusion.

Together they form the human foundation of the tool shed domain. Their differing motivations and behaviors show where the system must be simple, where it must be reliable, and where it must support growing confidence.
